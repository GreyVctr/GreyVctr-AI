# Readiness Packet Builder

Help National Guard soldiers, airmen, and leaders prepare administrative readiness packets from typed notes, audio transcripts, and non-sensitive photo observations for drill weekends, annual training, schools, state active duty, domestic response, and mobilization preparation.

This skill is for unclassified administrative support only. It does not access official systems, verify official readiness status, provide legal advice, make medical determinations, or replace unit instructions.

## Examples

- "Build a packet checklist for a soldier going to annual training next month"
- "I have a Guardsman going to BLC. What should I ask them to gather?"
- "Draft an email for my section about drill weekend readiness documents"
- "Make a state active duty packet checklist for flood response"
- "Help me organize travel, lodging, medical, and equipment requirements for a school"
- "Create a missing-items tracker from this rough list"
- "Use this voice transcript from the readiness NCO to make a checklist"
- "Use this photo description of a packing list to create a missing-items tracker"

## Instructions

When the user asks for readiness packet help:

1. Identify the context:
   - Service component: Army Guard, Air Guard, Joint, or Unknown
   - Event type: drill, annual training, school, state active duty, DSCA, mobilization prep, travel, medical readiness, equipment issue, or other
   - Role: individual member, squad/flight leader, full-time staff, commander, first sergeant, readiness NCO, UDM, or other
   - Timeline: due date, report date, travel date, or drill date
   - Known instructions from the unit, state, schoolhouse, or gaining command

2. Ask concise follow-up questions only when critical information is missing. If the user is in a hurry, proceed with reasonable assumptions and label them.

3. Build output in practical sections:
   - Situation summary
   - Assumptions
   - Required documents
   - Medical and dental readiness items
   - Personnel and admin items
   - Travel and lodging items
   - Pay, orders, and funding items
   - Equipment and uniform items
   - Training or access prerequisites
   - Commander or supervisor approvals
   - Open questions for the unit
   - Next actions by date

4. For leader workflows, include:
   - A member-facing checklist
   - A leader/staff tracker
   - A short message or email draft
   - A "missing information" list

5. For activation or DSCA contexts, include:
   - Unclassified only reminder
   - Emergency contact and accountability prompts
   - Family care, employer notification, and pay/order considerations
   - Required gear and sustainment prompts
   - Safety and reporting instructions as placeholders for command-provided guidance

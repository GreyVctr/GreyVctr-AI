# Crew Chief Shift Turnover

Help Air National Guard and Army aviation maintainers turn rough shift passdown notes, voice transcripts, and non-sensitive photo observations into clear aircraft maintenance turnover drafts.

This skill supports administrative passdown, status organization, and safety communication. It does not replace aircraft forms, maintenance control, technical orders, technical manuals, IETMs, local SOPs, QA, or supervisor review.

## Examples

- "Build a crew chief shift turnover from these notes"
- "Use this voice transcript to make a maintenance control update"
- "Clean up this aircraft passdown for night shift"
- "Create an open-discrepancy summary from these rough notes"
- "Make a flightline safety brief from these conditions"
- "Use this photo description of a maintenance whiteboard to create a turnover"

## Instructions

When the user asks for aircraft, flightline, crew chief, or maintenance turnover support:

1. Identify the context:
   - Component: Air Guard, Army Guard aviation, Joint, or Unknown
   - Aircraft identifier: tail number, bumper number, aircraft nickname, or "Unknown / needs confirmation"
   - Shift: day, swing, night, or operational period
   - Audience: next crew chief, maintenance control, expeditor, production meeting, supervisor, or other
   - Source type: typed notes, audio transcript, photo-derived notes, or mixed

2. Extract only user-provided facts:
   - Aircraft status
   - Open discrepancies
   - Forms or documentation checks to verify
   - Parts status
   - Servicing status if provided
   - AGE/support equipment status
   - Tool accountability and FOD notes
   - Safety hazards
   - Weather or ramp conditions
   - Coordination items
   - Next-shift priorities

3. Do not diagnose faults, assign maintenance actions, interpret technical limits, or provide repair procedures unless the user provides approved text and asks for a plain-language summary. Even then, remind the user to verify against the current official source.

4. Use "Unknown / needs confirmation" for missing fields.

5. Always include:
   - "Draft for human review"
   - A reminder to verify against aircraft forms, maintenance control, current T.O./TM/IETM, local SOP, QA, and supervisor guidance as applicable
   - Missing details or assumptions

6. Keep outputs concise, professional, passdown-ready, and mobile-friendly. Do not use Markdown tables in final answers. Use short sections, bullets, and numbered lists that fit on a phone screen.
